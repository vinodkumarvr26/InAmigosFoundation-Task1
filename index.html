Who We Are

Meet InAmigos Foundation

We believe in grassroot level transformation and empowerment.

InAmigos Foundation is a Section 8 registered non-profit organization dedicated to creating sustainable social impact through education, women empowerment, environmental sustainability, humanitarian initiatives, animal welfare, and youth development programs.

Our Mission

To address immediate social struggles by designing local solutions that foster education, gender equality, environmental stability, and compassionate care.

Our Vision

An inclusive and green India where every individual can lead a life of dignity, opportunity, self-sufficiency, and co-exist in harmony with nature and wildlife.

Our Values

Compassion in action, transparency in execution, innovation in sustainability, and absolute dedication to serving communities without any discrimination.

Transformation Stories

Lives Transformed

Real stories of change, strength, and achievement made possible by your continuous support.
Education Path

Aarav's Journey to School

Born in a remote hamlet in Maharashtra, Aarav had to drop out to help his parents. Project Bachpanshala provided books, financial support, and a counseling session that persuaded his parents to restart his formal schooling. Aarav now ranks top in his science class.

Skill Empowerment

Priya's Financial Wings

Priya was struggling to support her family of five. Joining Project Udaan's sewing workshops gave her professional-grade tailoring training. Today, she runs a micro-boutique from her home, employs two local women, and pays for her siblings' education.

Youth Leadership

Karan's Development Spark

As a collegiate intern under Project Vikas, Karan organized large waste segregation and tree plantation campaigns. The hand-on leadership experience built his public speaking skills, culminating in his placement at a global sustainability firm.

Green Initiative

Green Roots in Bhopal

An entire neighborhood was facing severe pollution and summer heat index spikes. Project Prakriti volunteers coordinated a local plantation campaign, planting 1,200 native species. The local community now manages a clean park with a self-sustained drip watering model.
